Indian power system is complex with many operating constraints and one them being pollution flashover of insulators. At voltage level above 220 kV, the pollution flashover of the insulators plays a critical role in the reliable power transmission, in this regard, the project has a greater potential in catering need of the Indian power sector. The high success rate of polymeric insulator is majorly attributed to the hydrophobicity property which provides the excellent shielding against the pollution flashover. However, with time, polymeric insulators undergo degradation due to which they turn into hydrophilic thereby reducing its strength against flash-over. For Indian operating and environmental conditions, the performance of these insulators is still to be examined carefully, having been observed the failures in service condition. The project aims at developing superhydrophobic insulators with less flashover rates.

High voltage or high current subsystems are part of any high power transmitting radar. Earthing is essential for a safe and reliable power system. Methods to improve radar subsystem grounding with supporting measurements of earth resistance, and computational models to simulate the possible Ground Potential Rise (GPR) due to injected current surges are of particular interest. The main objective of the project work is to develop a reliable computational model for the impedance of insulation earthing rods and the distribution of the surface potential that occurs when a power frequency fault current is injected into the earthing system. This work also involves the frequency dependent modeling of the soil impedance.

The study aims at understanding the interfacial properties and surface behaviour due to the deposition of the surface charges, application of surface charging in addressing the societal problems like pollution control, water purification through phase separation using corona discharges etc. The corona discharges being faint and falls mostly in the UV spectral range 200 nm- 350 nm, has the very weak emission in the visible range. Therefore, the application of digital image processing is possible to understand these discharges in deeper sense. As an extension image processing can be thought of non-destructive plasma diagnosing tool.

CO2 is one of the greenhouse gases extensively found in the atmosphere. It has been recognized worldwide as a main contributor for the global warming. Most of the CO2 has been generated by the various emission sources like thermal power plants, automobiles etc. Present day challenge is to reduce the amount of CO2 produced by the emission process. The electrochemical processes are well known to reduce the gases CO2 to liquid/solid products. Here we propose to charge the neutral CO2 using the corona discharge method which will ease the electrochemical reduction of CO2. Objective is to utilize the corona discharge procedure to reduce CO2 to economically valuable products.
